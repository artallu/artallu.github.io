About Me

Hello! I am Dr. Raviteja Allu, a postdoctoral researcher at Trinity College Dublin. I am a postgraduate fellow at CONNECT, focusing on next-generation wireless communications, including cognitive radio, backscatter communications, integrated sensing and communications (ISAC), and MIMO systems. Currently working on the prototype implementation of Orthogonal Time Frequency Space (OTFS) modulation

Experience & Education

Work Experience

Postdoctoral Researcher

Dec, 2024 - Present | Trinity College Dublin

My research focuses on implementing the prototype for the Orthogonal Time Frequency Space and study the resource allocation strategies for the Next generation wireless communication systems which includes the next generation multiple access techniques like NOMA, RSMA, etc.. in ISAC.

Visiting Research Scholar

Jan, 2024 - May, 2024 | Temasek Laboratory, NTU, Singapore

During this period, I worked as a visiting research scholar at Temasek Laboratory, NTU, Singapore. In NTU, I worked on the prototyping of Reconfigurable Intelligent surfaces and its applications in passive localization.

Education

Ph.D. in Institute of Communications Engineering

Feb, 2021 - Sep, 2024 | National Sun Yat-sen University, Taiwan

My doctoral research contributed significantly to the field of wireless communications. I worked on projects like Integrated sensing and communication systems enhanced and reconfigurable with intelligent surface assistance using next-generation multi-access technology, and 6G mid and high-frequency high-order MIMO mobile communication system.

M.Tech. in Electrical Engineering

Sept, 2017 - May, 2019 | National Institute of Technology Rourkela, India

My research centered on the development of a tracking controller for autonomous VTOL aircraft, employing recurrent neural networks to optimize landing on aircraft carriers using SIMULINK.
